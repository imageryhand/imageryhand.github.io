Arte, scientia, fantasia. Skill, knowledge, imagination - the three ingredients Leonardo Da Vinci believed good art should have. That's what I strive to achieve. My paintings are created by hand using a fusion of traditional Italian Renaissance and Flemish oil painting methods. My tools: ox and hog hair paintrushes, gesso primed canvas, vine charcoal sticks, walnut and linseed oils for thinning. I have about a dozen paint tubes: two sets of primaries, the Earth tones, plus black and white. Books have guided me on my artistic journey. I derive many of my techniques and recipes from antique painting manuals, some of them many centuries old, as well as trial and error in my personal practice. I wish to follow the Old Masters as faithfully as I can, and I do not care if some critics out there consider such an approach outdated and unstylish.
